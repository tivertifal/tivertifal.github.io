Making a traditional roguelike

Writing larger code projects always involves moments where I realize that the way I've done something isn't ideal, and that my previous solutions and decisions are now either blocking me completely, or making something I want to do unnecessarily complicated. When this happens, I have to go back and rewrite stuff. Sometimes it's a minor issue that I can solve by, say, splitting a property of an object up into multiple properties. But sometimes it's a bigger issue that forces me to dramatically change how I go about doing something by rewriting large parts of the codebase.

Backtracking and refactoring is impossible to prevent completely, but I want to minimize it as much as possible. The trick, I think, is to find a sensible balance between getting stuck in the planning stages in an attempt to predict all potential challenges and pitfalls on the one hand, and just happily coding away without any sort of forethought on the other.

The purpose of these pages is to help me get through the planning stages. By forcing myself to write down my thoughts in a coherent, linear form, I make sure that I have everything sorted into the right boxes and categories, and that I understand everything well enough to describe how it works. My goal is to end up with some design principles that can guide my hand in the execution phase of the project.
