Making a traditional roguelike

These pages are about making a traditional roguelike and my thoughts along the way.

Writing larger code projects has historically, for me personally, involved a lot of moments where I've realized the way I've done something isn't ideal, and my previous solutions and decisions are now either blocking me completely, or making something I want to do unnecessarily complicated. When this happens, I have to go back and rewrite stuff. Sometimes it's a minor issue, like splitting a property of an object up into multiple properties. Sometimes it's a bigger issue, where I have to dramatically change how I go about doing something by rewriting large parts of the codebase.

I think this sort of backtracking and refactoring is impossible to prevent completely, but I want to minimize it as much as possible. The trick, I think, is to find a sensible balance between getting stuck in the planning stages in an attempt to predict all potential challenges and pitfalls on the one hand, and coding away without any sort of forethought on the other.

The purpose of these pages isn't so much to help anyone else, but because I know that by having to put down my thoughts in a coherent, linear form, I have to think about stuff in a particular way, and I've learnt that this way of thinking is really useful to me. When I have to explain something in this way, I have to get everything sorted into the right boxes and categories, and I have to understand everything well enough to pass on that knowledge. So essentially I'm writing this to help myself.
